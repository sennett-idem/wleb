Resource Specialist

The ISDA, Division of Soil Conservation (DSC) employs Resource Specialists (RSs) throughout the state of Indiana to assist landowners and stakeholders in the watershed to provide agronomic technical assistance focused on soil health and water quality, develop conservation plans, and design and implement conservation BMPs through the local, state and federal conservation programs available. The RSs also serve and support the SWCDs by assisting with conservation activities and outreach mailings, helping to carry out conservation programs, reporting accomplishments, planning and assisting with field days/workshops, and educating landowners, school-age children and the public on the importance of conservation and soil health. There are four Resource Specialist Team Leaders that are responsible for overseeing the responsibilities and daily duties of the DSC Resource Specialists located in their respective areas, in addition to performing the regular duties of an RS as explained above.

District Support Specialist

The ISDA, Division of Soil Conservation employs District Support Specialists (DSSs) that work cooperatively throughout the state with Soil and Water Conservation Districts (SWCDs) and other conservations partners in the design of programs that reach landowners, the general public, and governmental officials on the husbandry and management of soil and water resources. They prepare and conduct training for SWCD supervisors and staff, assist SWCDs in expanding their capacity to fulfill their role in their communities, provide facilitation for strategic planning and similar sessions, and provide information, guidance and direct on-site assistance to SWCDs in carrying out their legal and operational responsibilities.
